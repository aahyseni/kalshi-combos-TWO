EXECUTIVE VERDICT
-----------------

The implementation is substantially improved, but it is not yet fully validated
to balance the live book correctly or accept only correctly measured +EV hedges.

Most prior audit items are genuinely implemented. The analytic mutex-aware
directional bound is a reasonable conservative balancing control, and the
candidate gate requires positive expected value under the production model.
However, two P0 correctness problems remain in the candidate gate:

1. Candidate/reservation premiums are incorrectly added to the P(ruin) equity
   basis before they have been paid, understating post-fill ruin risk.
2. Candidate MC runs before reservation and has no reservation-version validation,
   allowing concurrent accepts to evaluate against the same old pre-book.

The candidate gate has also processed zero real won auctions since relaunch, so
its live latency, confirmation behavior, balancing decisions, and fill outcomes
remain unverified.

Safe current description:

  "The engine is balancing-aware and production-model-positive-EV-gated in
   synthetic tests, but candidate admission is not yet fully atomic or live-
   validated."

Do not raise max_open_quotes, directional limits, or gross limits until the P0
items below are fixed and a real tiny-size candidate is observed end-to-end.

CONFIRMED FIXES
---------------

1. SQL rehydration fanout
- Fill aggregation and RFQ metadata de-duplication now occur before their join.
- Repeated RFQ tape rows no longer multiply position quantity.
- The regression test covers repeated RFQs.
- Live quantities/deltas returned to plausible levels and quoting resumed.

2. Exact exchange quantity
- Exchange position_fp, side, and query-level subaccount are authoritative.
- Local records provide cost basis and leg provenance.

3. Unmodeled holdings
- Gated or missing-marginal holdings remain as deterministic premium/gross
  reserves rather than disappearing or being sampled at fabricated p=0.5.

4. Fractional contracts
- Whole-contract flooring and the forced one-contract minimum were removed from
  the updated MC position path.

5. ES versus deterministic maximum
- Sampled model ES and deterministic maximum loss now have separate fields and
  separate gates.

6. Position generations
- Risk snapshots carry exposure generations.
- Position mutations invalidate older results.
- Off-loop results are discarded when their generation is stale.

7. Challenger correlation scope
- Correlation inflation is restricted to same-game pairs rather than forcing
  cross-game rho=0 to rho=0.5.

8. Structural/fallback handling
- Conditioned fallback behavior exists where a defensible shared link is known.
- Independent-split and full-copula challengers remain conservative backstops.

9. Off-loop book risk
- Full-book MC is run off the event loop, addressing the observed heartbeat
  starvation from inline MC.

10. Candidate EV sign
- Live candidate configuration sets:
    allow_negative_ev_hedge = False
    hedge_cost_budget_cc = 0
- Negative-EV candidates are therefore declined.
- The bot is not currently authorized to buy negative-EV insurance solely for
  risk reduction.

P0-1: CANDIDATE P(RUIN) EQUITY BASIS IS OVERSTATED
-------------------------------------------------

Observed implementation:

The candidate input builder constructs one merged model containing:
- committed positions;
- outstanding reservations;
- the contemplated candidate.

It then calculates the ruin equity basis using:

  current available cash
  + modeled cost basis of the merged model

The merged modeled cost basis includes reservation and candidate premiums even
though those premiums have not yet been debited from current cash.

The candidate P&L sample already contains:

  candidate terminal payout - candidate entry price

Adding candidate entry price to pre-fill cash and then applying payout-price
cancels the candidate cost incorrectly:

  current cash + candidate price + (candidate payout - candidate price)
  = current cash + candidate payout

The correct post-fill terminal value is:

  current cash + candidate payout - candidate price

The existing calculation therefore overstates post-candidate equity by the
candidate premium. Outstanding reservations produce the same overstatement.

Impact:
- Post-candidate P(ruin) is understated.
- Larger/more expensive candidates receive more erroneous equity credit.
- Multiple outstanding reservations compound the problem.

Required fix:
- Build the equity basis from committed modeled positions only:

    current available cash
    + cost basis of committed modeled positions

- Do not add uncommitted reservation or candidate premiums to that basis.
- Let reservation/candidate sampled P&L include their payout minus purchase cost.
- Alternatively subtract candidate/reservation premiums from cash before adding
  their cost basis, but committed-only basis is simpler and less error-prone.

Required tests:
- One candidate, both terminal outcomes.
- Multiple outstanding reservations.
- Prove exactly:

    post terminal equity
    = current cash
    + terminal value of committed holdings
    + candidate terminal payout
    - candidate premium

- Verify P(ruin) increases appropriately with candidate price at fixed payout
  distribution.

P0-2: CANDIDATE MC IS NOT ATOMIC WITH RESERVATIONS
--------------------------------------------------

Observed confirm order:

  analytic last-look decision
  -> build candidate MC inputs from current reservations
  -> await candidate MC worker
  -> fill-velocity check
  -> create risk reservation
  -> send confirm

The candidate MC awaits a worker before creating its reservation. During this
await, another accepted quote can begin its own candidate evaluation.

Unsafe sequence:

  Accept A snapshots zero reservations
  Accept B snapshots zero reservations
  A and B run MC concurrently
  A passes against the old book
  B passes against the same old book
  A reserves
  B reserves later

The later analytic reservation check still protects existing hard analytic and
mass-acceptance caps, but B's ES/P(ruin) decision did not include A. I found no
reservation_version in CandidateBookRiskInputs and no version comparison/retry
after the worker returns.

Impact:
- Candidate ES and P(ruin) admission are not atomic under simultaneous accepts.
- Multiple candidates can independently pass even if their combined model tail
  breaches the portfolio budget.

Required fix, preferred flow:

1. Create a provisional reservation under the analytic hard caps.
2. Capture ExposureBook generation and RiskReservationService version.
3. Run candidate MC with all reservations, including the provisional candidate.
4. On worker return, verify both generation and reservation version.
5. If either changed, rebuild and retry within a bounded confirm deadline.
6. If MC declines/errors/times out, release the provisional reservation.
7. If MC passes, proceed to exchange confirm.

Alternative:
- Serialize candidate-risk admissions through one queue. This is simpler but can
  reduce throughput and must still respect the exchange confirm deadline.

Required tests:
- Two candidate gates launched concurrently cannot both ignore each other.
- Reservation added during MC causes discard/retry.
- Reservation release changes the version and causes reevaluation.
- Combined candidates exceeding ES/P(ruin) admit at most the safe subset.
- Provisional reservation is released on MC error, timeout, or decline.

P1: "+EV" IS PRODUCTION-MODEL EV, NOT ROBUST EV
------------------------------------------------

Candidate EV is calculated as:

  post production EV - pre production EV

Challenger, bridge, independent-split, and parameter-stress paths affect
governing ES and P(ruin), but they do not govern candidate EV.

Therefore a candidate may be positive EV under the production structural model
while negative under one or more challengers.

This is not necessarily wrong; a maker normally trades from a primary fair
model. But documentation should say:

  "positive expected value under the production model"

not robustly +EV under all credible model uncertainty.

Recommended improvement:
- Calculate and log:
    production candidate EV;
    bridge candidate EV;
    parameter-challenger candidate EV;
    worst credible candidate EV.
- Suggested policy:

    production_candidate_ev > 0
    AND worst_challenger_ev >= configurable negative tolerance

- Do not require every extreme stress to be positive EV unless the operator
  explicitly chooses that highly conservative policy.

BOOK-BALANCING VALIDATION
-------------------------

Analytic directional balancing:

The mutex-aware directional calculation is a defensible conservative improvement:
- one explicitly mutually exclusive result event is recognized;
- opposing advances fall into separate branches;
- maximum branch exposure replaces the sum of incompatible branches;
- adding entries cannot lower the bound;
- zero or multiple ME dimensions fail closed;
- the original loose delta_by_game backstop remains separately enforced.

This gives justified credit to opposing ARG/ENG advance exposure while retaining
mass-acceptance subset dominance.

Caveat:
- This is not exact net delta.
- It is a branchwise upper bound built from independence-proxy magnitudes.
- Call it a "mutex-aware directional bound," not exact portfolio direction.

MC balancing:

The structural sampler places opposing advance outcomes in a shared game state,
so a usable MC should recognize that both cannot resolve adverse simultaneously.

But current validation is incomplete because:
- concurrent candidates can omit each other;
- candidate P(ruin) uses an overstated equity basis;
- the candidate gate has not run on a real won auction;
- the loose hard max_event_delta proxy can still block balancing flow even when
  the percentage directional cap gives hedge credit.

Conclusion:
- The architecture is balancing-aware.
- Synthetic tests indicate it can credit balancing positions.
- Live behavior has not yet proven that it admits the correct balancing fills.

LIVE CANDIDATE-GATE LATENCY
---------------------------

The candidate gate runs during Kalshi's short confirm window and awaits an
approximately 20,000-sample off-process calculation. Off-loop execution protects
the heartbeat but does not guarantee that the confirm deadline is met.

The report contains no live candidate-gate latency because no won auction has
reached the gate since relaunch.

Required metrics:
- MC worker queue dwell;
- candidate-gate p50/p90/p99 runtime;
- remaining confirm-window time at completion;
- gate timeouts/errors;
- version-conflict retries;
- accepts lost because the exchange window expired before confirm.

Required behavior:
- Bound retries by the actual remaining confirm deadline.
- Fail closed when insufficient time remains.
- Do not allow risk computation to silently consume the whole confirmation
  window.

DOCUMENTATION CORRECTIONS
-------------------------

The full-session master report should not state or imply that live +EV balancing
is fully validated. Replace with:

  "The confirm path requires positive production-model candidate EV and post-book
   risk limits. Synthetic tests show balancing candidates can receive MC credit.
   The gate has not yet processed a live won auction, and reservation concurrency
   plus candidate-equity accounting require correction before full validation."

The claim that all P0 items are green is too strong until:
- candidate/reservation admission is atomic;
- candidate/reservation premiums are handled correctly in ruin equity;
- the live candidate gate processes a real auction win within deadline.

REQUIRED IMPLEMENTATION ORDER
-----------------------------

1. Fix candidate/reservation equity-basis accounting.
2. Add reservation-version validation or provisional-reservation ordering.
3. Add concurrent candidate-admission tests.
4. Log production and challenger candidate EV separately.
5. Add candidate-gate latency/deadline metrics.
6. Exercise one real won auction at tiny size.
7. Verify from logs:
    candidate EV was positive under the production model;
    all outstanding reservations were included;
    exposure generation and reservation version matched;
    post ES/P(ruin)/deterministic/gross budgets passed;
    reservation succeeded;
    confirm landed inside the exchange window;
    resulting position changed branchwise risk as predicted.
8. Only then reconsider max_open_quotes or directional thresholds.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-------------------

The live balancing/+EV claim is validated only when:
- candidate ruin equity exactly accounts for all premiums once and only once;
- concurrent accepts cannot use the same stale reservation state;
- candidate MC and reservation admission form one version-checked transaction;
- production candidate EV is positive and challenger EV is visible;
- post-book ES/P(ruin) includes committed holdings, reservations, and candidate;
- candidate computation completes within the exchange confirm deadline;
- at least one real tiny-size won auction passes the gate, confirms, executes,
  reconciles, and moves risk in the predicted balancing direction;
- concentrating and balancing live examples produce the correct opposite
  decisions under identical cap configuration.

FINAL STATUS
------------

Most of the audit plan was implemented competently, and analytic balancing is
substantially improved. Two P0 candidate-gate defects remain. Until fixed and
live-exercised, describe the system as:

  "Balancing-aware and production-model-positive-EV-gated in tests, not yet
   fully validated for atomic live candidate admission."
