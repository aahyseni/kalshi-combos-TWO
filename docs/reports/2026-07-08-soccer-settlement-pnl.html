The headline finding

P&L peaks at ~1¢ markup and falls as we mark up more — while the YES-hit rate of our fills climbs monotonically 1.3% → 14.8%. Raising the ask filters our fills down to exactly the combos the market priced high because they were genuinely likelier to hit — and they do. Fatter markup ⇒ more adverse selection ⇒ a more toxic book. For a parlay seller against 93%-busting flow, thin markup + broad volume beats fat markup + thin volume.

Audit — why removing UCL swung the number $27k (verified)

172 resolved = 123 WC + 49 UCL. P&L reconciles to the cent: WC +$6,660 + UCL +$26,936 = +$33,596. ~75% of the swing is a single UCL SPREAD+TOTAL combo — +$25,196 on 107,781 contracts, settled NO. We gate UCL off (two-legged-tie settlement we don't model); even ungated our fair was 77¢ vs 23¢ clearing, so we'd have won none of it. Excluding it hides no money we'd have made.

Method

  • Settlement via public GET /markets/{ticker} (status/result); combo settles YES iff every leg's result matches its selected side; eligible only if all legs resolved.
  • Our fair = live soccer engine at the marginal snapshot nearest each trade; we win iff our_ask ≤ clearing. Sell-parlay book only (taker_side=yes, 97% of flow).
  • Sample is early-round WC; 1,304 combos still pending. Directional, not final.
